What kind of app do you want to build?

This shapes everything — architecture, complexity, and what we tackle first

A

12V Guardian — Focused BM6 Dashboard

A single-purpose app laser-focused on the 12V battery problem. Real-time voltage gauge, history chart, alerts. OBD-II is a "nice to have" tab you'd add later. Think: a beautiful battery widget you glance at.

Pros

  • Ship fast, solve the real problem
  • Minimal dependencies (no ELM327 stack)
  • Perfect for open-source — focused scope

Cons

  • Veepeak sits unused in the app
  • Two apps to check your car
B

TucsonKit — Unified Vehicle Monitor

One app, two radios. BM6 tab for always-on 12V monitoring, OBD-II tab for periodic deep diagnostics. Both share a vehicle profile and history. The open-source "PHEV Watchdog for iOS" that doesn't exist yet.

Pros

  • One app for everything
  • Cross-reference 12V data with traction battery
  • Fills a real gap (no iOS PHEV tool exists)

Cons

  • Larger scope, longer to ship
  • Tucson PHEV PIDs need reverse engineering
  • SwiftOBD2 dependency to evaluate
C

HyundaiEV — Platform for Hyundai/Kia PHEVs

Think bigger: a general-purpose open-source diagnostic app for all Hyundai/Kia PHEVs and EVs. Pluggable PID packs per vehicle model. BM6 support as a plugin. Community-contributed vehicle profiles.

Pros

  • Maximum community impact
  • Could become the LeafSpy for Hyundai
  • Extensible architecture pays off long-term

Cons

  • Massive scope — months not weeks
  • Need vehicle PIDs from many models
  • Risk of never shipping
